Phase 1: Foundation & Development (T1 Translation)
Year 1 (Jan 2026 - Dec 2026) | Budget: $750,000
Ethics & Governance Framework Development
Obtain HREC ethics approval for statewide liquid biopsy biobank. Establish governance framework for all participating sites including data management protocols, patient consent procedures, and sample tracking systems.
Timeline: Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar)
NSW Health Statewide Biobank Partnership
Establish formal partnership with NSW Health Statewide Biobank. Set up infrastructure for sample collection, processing, and storage. Implement ISO 9001 quality standards.
Timeline: Q2 2026 (Apr-Jun)
Standard Operating Procedures Development
Develop comprehensive SOPs for blood collection, plasma separation, cfDNA extraction, and sample storage. Create training materials and certification programs for collection center staff.
Timeline: Q2 2026 (Apr-Jun)
Fragmentomics Protocol Development
Develop novel fragmentomics assays leveraging machine learning algorithms. Establish ctDNA/RNA extraction protocols with sensitivity targets of <0.1% mutant allele frequency.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2026 (Jul-Dec)
AI/Machine Learning Platform Development
Build AI/ML infrastructure for fragment pattern analysis. Train algorithms on existing cancer datasets. Develop predictive models for multi-cancer early detection.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2026 (Jul-Dec)
ESR1 Mutation Testing Implementation
Implement ESR1 mutation testing for metastatic breast cancer patients. Target $55 disposable cost per test. Prepare documentation for Medicare rebate application (MBS item).
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2026 (Jul-Dec)
NSCLC Panel Development
Develop comprehensive NSCLC liquid biopsy panel covering EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF, MET, RET, and NTRK alterations. Validate against tissue biopsy standards.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2026 (Jul-Dec)
AGRF Partnership Establishment
Formalize partnership with Australian Genome Research Facility for initial sequencing capacity. Establish data transfer protocols and quality metrics.
Timeline: Q2 2026 (Apr-Jun)
Staff Recruitment & Training Program
Recruit 2 postdoctoral researchers, 3 PhD students, and 4 technical staff. Initiate comprehensive training program on liquid biopsy techniques and interpretation.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2026 (Jan-Jun)
Consumer Advisory Committee Formation
Establish Consumer Governance Committee with 4 patient representatives and 3 clinical investigators. Develop patient engagement framework and communication materials.
Timeline: Q2 2026 (Apr-Jun)
Phase 2: Infrastructure & Validation (T2 Translation)
Years 2-3 (Jan 2027 - Dec 2028) | Budget: $1,500,000
Next-Generation Sequencing Platform Procurement
Purchase and install Illumina NovaSeq 6000 and NextSeq 2000 platforms at Kolling Institute. Establish local sequencing capacity to eliminate dependence on Victorian facilities.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2027 (Jan-Jun)
Automated cfDNA Extraction Systems
Install QIASymphony SP/AS automated extraction systems. Validate high-throughput processing protocols for 200+ samples per day. Achieve 24-hour turnaround capability.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2027 (Jan-Jun)
Digital PCR Platform Installation
Install Bio-Rad QX200 digital PCR systems for ultra-sensitive mutation detection. Validate for clinical use with detection limits of 0.01% mutant allele frequency.
Timeline: Q2 2027 (Apr-Jun)
Laboratory Information Management System
Implement LIMS for sample tracking, workflow management, and results reporting. Integrate with NSW Health Pathology systems and electronic medical records.
Timeline: Q3 2027 (Jul-Sep)
Clinical Validation Study - Breast Cancer
Validate ESR1 mutation testing in 500 metastatic breast cancer patients. Compare concordance with tissue biopsy. Establish clinical decision thresholds.
Timeline: Q4 2027 - Q2 2028 (Oct 2027 - Jun 2028)
Clinical Validation Study - Lung Cancer
Validate NSCLC panel in 800 patients. Demonstrate 95% concordance with tissue testing. Document improved detection of resistance mutations.
Timeline: Q1-Q3 2028 (Jan-Sep 2028)
Colorectal Cancer Panel Development
Develop liquid biopsy panel for colorectal cancer including KRAS, NRAS, BRAF, and microsatellite instability detection. Validate for treatment selection and monitoring.
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2028 (Apr-Dec 2028)
Multi-Cancer Early Detection Development
Develop MCED assay combining fragmentomics, methylomics, and genomics. Target detection of 10+ cancer types with 99.5% specificity.
Timeline: Q3 2027 - Q4 2028 (Jul 2027 - Dec 2028)
Minimal Residual Disease Assay Development
Develop tumor-informed MRD assays for post-surgical monitoring. Validate in 300 patients across multiple cancer types. Establish recurrence prediction models.
Timeline: Q1-Q4 2028 (Jan-Dec 2028)
Quality Management System Implementation
Implement ISO 15189 medical laboratory quality standards. Develop internal quality control programs and participate in external quality assessment schemes.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2028 (Jul-Dec 2028)
Bioinformatics Pipeline Development
Develop automated analysis pipelines for variant calling, fragment analysis, and clinical interpretation. Implement cloud-based computing infrastructure.
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2027 (Apr-Dec 2027)
Memorial Sloan Kettering Collaboration
Establish research collaboration with MSK-ACCESS liquid biopsy program. Exchange protocols and validate international standards.
Timeline: Q1 2028 (Jan-Mar 2028)
Phase 3: Accreditation & Clinical Integration
Years 3-4 (Jan 2028 - Dec 2029) | Budget: $1,500,000
NATA Accreditation Preparation
Prepare comprehensive documentation for NATA accreditation including validation studies, quality procedures, and proficiency testing results.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2029 (Jan-Jun)
NATA Assessment & Accreditation
Complete NATA assessment process. Achieve accreditation for liquid biopsy testing enabling Medicare billing eligibility.
Timeline: Q2-Q3 2029 (Apr-Sep)
Clinical Trial Integration - AGITG Studies
Partner with Australasian Gastro-Intestinal Trials Group to add liquid biopsy arms to 3 active trials. Enroll 200+ GI cancer patients.
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2029 (Apr-Dec)
Clinical Trial Integration - Breast Cancer
Integrate liquid biopsy monitoring into BCT-ANZ trials. Focus on treatment resistance monitoring and early relapse detection.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2029 (Jul-Dec)
Clinical Trial Integration - Lung Cancer
Partner with ALTG for liquid biopsy integration in immunotherapy trials. Monitor response and resistance to checkpoint inhibitors.
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2029 (Apr-Dec)
Healthcare Professional Training Program
Train 100+ oncologists and pathologists on liquid biopsy interpretation. Develop CPD-accredited education modules. Create clinical decision support tools.
Timeline: Q1-Q4 2029 (Jan-Dec)
MyLiquidBiopsy Digital Platform Development
Co-design patient portal with consumer committee. Features include result tracking, education resources, and clinician messaging.
Timeline: Q1-Q3 2029 (Jan-Sep)
Regional Champion Network Establishment
Recruit and train 15 regional consumer champions including Aboriginal health workers and culturally diverse community leaders.
Timeline: Q2-Q3 2029 (Apr-Sep)
Patient Education Materials Development
Create culturally appropriate education materials in Arabic, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Hindi, and English. Develop visual aids and video content.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2029 (Jan-Jun)
Medicare Application Preparation
Prepare MSAC applications for ESR1 and NSCLC liquid biopsy tests. Compile health economic data demonstrating cost-effectiveness.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2029 (Jul-Dec)
Pilot Implementation - Sydney LHD
Launch pilot liquid biopsy service at Royal Prince Alfred Hospital. Test ordering systems, sample logistics, and result reporting.
Timeline: Q4 2029 (Oct-Dec)
Phase 4: Statewide Implementation & Sustainability (T3 Translation)
Years 4-5 (Jan 2029 - Dec 2030) | Budget: $1,500,000
Northern NSW LHD Rollout
Implement liquid biopsy services across Northern NSW including Lismore, Tweed, and Grafton. Establish collection points at 50+ centers.
Timeline: Q1 2030 (Jan-Mar)
Western NSW LHD Rollout
Expand services to Western NSW including Dubbo, Orange, and Broken Hill. Implement sample transport logistics for remote areas.
Timeline: Q1-Q2 2030 (Jan-Jun)
Hunter New England LHD Rollout
Deploy services across Hunter New England region. Establish hub laboratory at John Hunter Hospital.
Timeline: Q2 2030 (Apr-Jun)
South Eastern Sydney & Illawarra Rollout
Complete statewide coverage with implementation in remaining Sydney metro and regional areas.
Timeline: Q2-Q3 2030 (Apr-Sep)
Medicare Item Number Implementation
Operationalize Medicare billing for approved liquid biopsy tests. Establish billing infrastructure and provider education.
Timeline: Q3 2030 (Jul-Sep)
Private Billing System Launch
Establish private patient billing for non-Medicare covered tests. Implement online payment systems and insurance interfaces.
Timeline: Q3 2030 (Jul-Sep)
Clinical Trial Support Service
Launch fee-for-service liquid biopsy support for pharmaceutical trials. Target 20+ active trials with 2000+ patients annually.
Timeline: Q2-Q4 2030 (Apr-Dec)
Performance Monitoring Dashboard
Launch real-time performance dashboard tracking turnaround times, quality metrics, and patient outcomes across all sites.
Timeline: Q1 2030 (Jan-Mar)
Health Economic Evaluation
Complete comprehensive health economic analysis demonstrating $50M annual savings through avoided biopsies and optimized treatments.
Timeline: Q3-Q4 2030 (Jul-Dec)
National Expansion Framework
Develop framework for national rollout including partnerships with interstate pathology providers and cancer centers.
Timeline: Q4 2030 (Oct-Dec)
Sustainability Achievement
Achieve financial break-even through triple revenue model. Document sustainability framework for ongoing operations.
Timeline: Q4 2030 (Oct-Dec)
